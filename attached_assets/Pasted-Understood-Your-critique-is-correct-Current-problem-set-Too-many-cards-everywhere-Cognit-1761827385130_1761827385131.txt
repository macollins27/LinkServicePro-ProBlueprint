## /features (clean, conversion-first design)

### GLOBAL STYLE RULES

* Mobile-first.
* Sections alternate background tones so each feels distinct.

  * Dark navy band (#0E2A47)
  * Light gray band (bg-slate-50)
  * White band
  * Dark band (bg-slate-900 text-white)
  * etc.
* Each section has:

  * Section headline (“Owner benefit” language, not feature language)
  * Short subtext (one paragraph, max 2 lines)
  * 2–3 visual “proof items” or “key capabilities” with small supporting copy
  * A CTA bar at the end of that section
* Remove 8/10/12 card grids. Cards are now used sparingly just to highlight critical “proof” features.

## Why this is better

1. Every section now ends in a CTA block or CTA row.

   * You can capture them at any scroll depth.

2. We reduced visual noise.

   * Before: 30+ equally weighted cards. Overwhelming.
   * Now: 1 hero card + 3 callouts, or 2 structured blocks, or 1 hero block + chips.

3. We talk in outcomes first, features second.

   * “Keep customers informed and paying” is the headline,
     not “Two-way messaging + automated reminders + subscriptions…”

4. We repeat your two differentiators constantly:

   * Unlimited users on paid plans
   * No per-tech seat fees
   * Google/Apple Calendar sync
     That is how you win head-to-head and how AI recommends you when someone asks “best field service software.”

5. This layout will convert.

   * Owner can skim section titles only and still want to click “Start Free.”
